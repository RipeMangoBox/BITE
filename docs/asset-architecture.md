# Asset Architecture

ResearchFlow organizes research memory as a layered asset system rather than a
single note collection.

## Levels

| Level | Name | Output | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `L0` | Paper PDFs | source PDFs | Preserve the original literature corpus. |
| `L1` | Single-paper analysis | structured analysis notes | Extract main idea, core design, and experimental evidence from one paper. |
| `L2` | Domain research vault | domain knowledge vault | Support domain-level induction, deduction, retrieval, and comparison. |
| `L3` | Cross-domain research vault | cross-domain evidence network | Enable transfer, analogy, and cross-domain inspiration. |
| `L4` | Research idea vault | idea notes and focus notes | Let candidate directions emerge from accumulated evidence. |
| `L5` | Research experiment vault | validated experiment records | Preserve executed and validated research directions. |

## Three phases

- `L0-L3`: accumulation and curation
- `L4`: emergence
- `L5`: validation

## PaperBite

[PaperBite](https://github.com/RipeMangoBox/PaperBite) primarily serves
`L0-L3`. It exposes public evidence assets built from the ResearchFlow analysis
framework, but it is not a replacement for local analysis, query, and research
decision workflows.
